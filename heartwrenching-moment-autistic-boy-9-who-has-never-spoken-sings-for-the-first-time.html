Heartwrenching moment autistic boy, 9, who has never spoken SINGS for the first time

 Posted on August 10, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  531 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

THIS IS the emotional moment a nine-year-old boy who had never spoken before sang for the first time at his school concert.

The remarkable moment Musab Mohamoud decided to speak for the first time was caught on camera during his school’s music week.

The nine-year-old has autism and complex learning difficulties, and had previously only mumbled words.

He had been classed as non-verbal, and relied on sign language to communicate his needs.

But the youngster amazed staff when he began forming the words of a song his classmates were learning during the special music week at Venturers’ Academy in Withywood, Bristol.

He then went on to perform the song alongside his classmates in front of a 100-strong audience – singing for the first time in his life.

Musab, who is a keen swimmer, also performed a solo of a song he had written on his own.

The nine-year-old has since started to form words properly, and even says “good morning” to his teachers when he arrives at school every day.

Speaking on the miraculous development, his dad, taxi driver Saeed, 40, of Barton Hill, Bristol, said: "It was a very special moment when Musab sang. It was like magic.

"He used to hum along to CBeebies when it came on the television but the school concert was the first time we had ever heard him properly sing,

"He is now starting to sing along to other songs when he recognises them on the TV.

"We are hoping his love of music and singing will help develop his communication skills.

"We have seen a real transformation in him since he started at the Venturers' Academy."

Venturers’ Academy is a state-funded school for kids with autism, which opened in September last year.

Its motto is “Where Everything is Possible.”

Headteacher Trystan Williams described the moment Musab burst out in song: "It was a truly incredible moment to see Musab and the others singing on the stage.

It was a very special moment when Musab sang. It was like magic.

Dad Saeed Mohamoud

"During the music week we noticed that Musab, who is non-verbal and previously had only mumbled words, was starting to form words.

"To see Musab stand on the stage and sing for the first time was an utterly magical and probably the most uplifting point in my 25 career of working with young people with learning difficulties."

Research has shown that neural pathways in the brain after different for singing and talking.

Mr Williams added that, after the music week in mid-January: "Musab is now starting to repeat things back to us.

"Him singing is the first step in encouraging him to speak independently.

"We are now working to build on his confidence to use his voice and develop his neural pathways.

"What Musab achieved was inspiring to our other students and parents and proves that if we look beyond the disability, anything is possible."

Pupils at Venturers’ Academy, including Musab, are set to record their school song with West End star Jonathan Wilkes.
