Brian Harman | Career

Brian started his professional career back in 2009 and has been one of the better players ever since.

In fact, the 36-year-old has won four titles during his 11 years as a professional to date.

To be precise, Harman won the 2010 Manor Classic, which was also his first professional tournament win.

Then, the Arizona native won the John Deere Classic in 2014. In doing so, he won his first PGA tournament. Thereafter, Harman had to wait for another three years before winning another tour.

That moment came in 2017 when Brian won the Wells Fargo Championship to win his second PGA Tour victory.

Furthermore, during the 18th and final hole, the 36-year-old slid home a 28-foot putt to win the championship by just one stroke over Dustin Johnson and Pat Perez.

Finally, his latest tour win came in 2018 when he won the QBE Shootout.

Other than that, Brian’s memorable moments throughout his career include becoming only the third player in the PGA Tour history to have two aces in the same round.
