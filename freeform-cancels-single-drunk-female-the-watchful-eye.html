FREEFORM has left a fan-favorite sitcom in the dust after only two short seasons.

Fans on social media are devastated by the sudden news.

The platform, which is owned by Disney, canceled two shows - Single Drunk Female and The Watchful Eye, according to Deadline.

Single Drunk Female was a comedy about an alcoholic woman in her 20s who is trying to get her life together.

She ends up moving back in with her mother while she finds herself.

The show starred Sofia Black-D’Elia and Ally Sheedy, the latter who is well-known for her role in The Breakfast Club.

Single Drunk Female wrapped its second and final season in April, and it's available to stream on Hulu.

Meanwhile, The Watchful Eye premiered in January, and it only ran for one season.

The contemporary thriller starred Mariel Molino who played a young woman working as a nanny for a rich family in New York City.

Kelly Bishop, the grandmother in the hit television show Gilmore Girls, also had a role in the show.

It was produced by ABC Signature, and Ryan Seacrest was an executive producer on the show.

END OF AN ERA

Fans of the shows on Twitter, especially Single Drunk Female, were deeply upset at this announcement.

One said: "What’s the point of even writing anymore when you don’t get to finish your projects?"

Someone else tweeted: "I liked Single Drunk Female, but shows I like always get canceled, so I guess I should apologize to the cast and crew."

"Oh, what do ya know another show I like is canceled. Single Drunk Female was funny... What a shame," added a third.

Another wrote: "This stinks. They were both really enjoyable shows."

"No!" someone else said while another user tweeted: "That's disappointing."

NOT SO GROWN

In March, Freeform announced the Black-ish spin-off will end with its upcoming sixth season.

Grown-ish stars Yara Shahidi, who played Zoey Johnson on Black-ish, following her as she leaves her family nest and heads to college.

In an emotional message posted on YouTube, Yara shared the news.

"Don’t be sad, because we’re going to do it up big," she encouraged. "We have so much to celebrate this season, including hitting our hundredth episode."

Yara also teased some special guest stars, including Lil Yachty and Anderson Pakk & The Free Nationals.

The final season will also be stretched over two years, with half of the episodes premiering this summer, and the rest coming out in 2024.
