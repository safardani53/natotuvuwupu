Family who rent our house are causing so much stress and are emotionally blackmailing us

 Posted on March 7, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  374 words  |  Larita Shotwell

DEAR DEIDRE: THE family who rent our house are causing us so much stress, we feel like packing our business up.

My wife and I run a property firm. We have a studio, which we did up, then we bought a flat and with the money we made on renting that, we bought a terraced house.

We thought we’d found ideal tenants, a couple with a teenage daughter.

Because we are nice people, they then started messing us around and didn’t pay us one month’s rent. We eventually got half.

I got a solicitor to write to them and they replied saying the stress we were causing was affecting their daughter’s school work.

It was like emotional blackmail. Another two months went by with no rent. Our solicitor wrote again giving them notice to leave.

Close to their leaving date, they phoned my wife saying if we make them homeless, they’ll have to move to a rough area and will be forced to give up their pets.

My wife said if they paid us what they owe, they could stay. We are still waiting. We are both 63 and we could do without this stress in our lives.

DEIDRE SAYS: You’ve given them plenty of opportunities to pay their rent. From what you’ve written they haven’t explained why they are behind or when they might be able to pay.

You have tried to be flexible but their debt is now mounting up without any prospect of them settling what they owe.

You can find out how to be firm but fair and process their eviction legally and properly through housingrights.org.uk.

It would be a shame to give up your business venture when you’ve worked so hard.

Using a lettings agent the next time to collect the rent may help ease these issues.
