LITTLE People, Big World alum Audrey Roloff has shown off her fit figure in a tiny swimsuit during a family vacation.

The reality star took to her Instagram Stories on Wednesday to share a slew of photos and videos from their trip.

Audrey, 31, and her husband, Jeremy, 33, took their three kids to Sauvie Island in Portland, Oregon.

The young mom captured snapshots of the youngsters playing with their toys in the sand and knee-deep in the water.

Their two sons, three-year-old Bode and one-year-old Radley seemed to stick together, while their five-year-old daughter, Ember, stayed close to dad.

The father-daughter pair posed for a photo while they were admiring the view from the beach.

They later joined the boys as Audrey shared some clips of Jeremy guiding the little ones across a huge log.

While Audrey appeared to be behind the camera in most of the posts, she did, at one point, film herself taking a dip in the water.

Sporting a black one-piece, strapless suit - differing from the religious star's typically conservative attire - Audrey walked into the water about thigh-deep before diving in head first.

She swam a few yards away before venturing back as her sons watched her from the sand.

Audrey then ran toward her boys while pulling the top of her bathing suit up to keep it in place.

CLAPPING BACK

Although the kids didn't appear to have spent much time swimming, Audrey recently defended her methods of teaching them how.

In an Instagram Story post, Audrey shared a photo of her youngest boy, Radley, floating on his back in an in-ground pool.

A swimming instructor was standing nearby, supervising the tot in the water.

Above the snap was a concerned followers' question about Infant Swimming Resource - a decades-old program that teaches young children survival skills in the water - which Audrey and her husband, Jeremy, have been using for their kids.

They wrote: "IRS seems super stressful for the kids, triggering their fight or flight survival. Thoughts?"

Audrey responded with a lengthy message explaining why she stands by the controversial program.

"Yes it can be. But it's also life saving. I don't think it's for everyone but if you spend a lot of time around pools, or you or a family member has a pool or pond or you live near a river/lake then you have to weigh the pros and cons yourself," the TLC alum began.

"My parents pool doesn't have a gate or cover and we spend a lot of time there especially now that we are neighbors so it makes me extremely nervous. This is a huge part of the reason why I looked into ISR in the first place."

She continued: "Our instructor is absolutely amazing with the kids even despite their resistance in the beginning. Don't buy into the stigma that it will make them be afraid of water or swimming forever. It won't."

'IRRESPONSIBLE'

Audrey's explanation came after she posted videos of her kids in the pool, immersed in the practice.

Although she cheered on her sons for their floating skills, she also warned viewers that the clips might be "triggering" to some.

Audrey and Jeremy have regularly come under fire for their parenting styles.

They recently were slammed by LPBW fans for allowing their children to play in a huge stagnant mud puddle.
