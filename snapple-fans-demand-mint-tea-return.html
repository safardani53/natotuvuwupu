Snapple fans rally to demand the return of 'delicious' drink years after company discontinued it

 Posted on March 5, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  447 words  |  Larita Shotwell

FANS are begging Snapple to bring back their favorite flavor of the popular tea, rallying across social media platforms to make their voice heard. 

Snapple offers a variety of flavors for their teas and pre-made juice drinks, though every now and then a fan-favorite is discontinued. 

One flavor avid-tea drinks are looking to get back on the shelves is Snapple’s Mint Iced Tea. 

The delicious drink was first introduced in 1987, according to the Daily Meal, and was adored for its fresh minty flavor. 

It was first discontinued in 2007, reportedly due to “soft sales and limited shelf space,” but appeared back in stores two years later. 

However, the flavor was quickly discontinued later that year.

While it has been nearly 15 years since fans have been able to enjoy the minty tea, Snapple has indicated it would be willing to restock the product. 

“There are no plans for a Mint Iced Tea revival tour at this point, Scott,” the company wrote in a May 2021 tweet responding to a fan asking to bring back the flavor. 

“But you never know when our nostalgic summer cravings might be too much. Keep letting us know which flavors you want to make a comeback, or keep around. We love to hear it!” 

On their website, Snapple details that customer input does “influence” decisions about extending limited flavors or bringing back a discontinued item.

Fans have appeared to pick up on that idea, turning to social media to express their love for the Mint Iced Tea. 

“@Snapple I stared drinking Snapple vanilla soda in 1972  then Mint iced tea I would fill my apartment with boxes of Snapple mint tea  it’s now 11 years without comeback please,” one customer wrote on Twitter. 

“This is just my desperate yearly tweet to @Snapple begging them to bring back the Mint Tea. Hi. Please?” another wrote. 

“if god is real and loves us why doesn’t snapple make mint tea anymore,” one joked. 

Meanwhile, on Facebook, one fan has created a page dedicated to bringing the flavor back to stores. 

The page, titled “Bring Back Snapple Mint Iced Tea,” has 270 likes and 275 followers. 

“THIS PAGE IS BEING CREATED FOR ALL OF US INCLUDING MYSELF THAT ARE IN FAVOR OF BRINGING BACK SNAPPLE MINT ICED TEA,” one post on the Facebook page reads. 

“Please....HELP ME GET SNAPPLE TO BRING BACK MINT SNAPPLE ICED TEA,” another reads.

It was not immediately clear why Snapple decided to shelve the fan-favorite drink for a second time 14 years ago. 

The company did not immediately respond to The US Sun’s request for comment.
