Lottie Moss breaks rules to party with Made In Chelsea pals Jamie Laing and Sophie Habboo during loc

 Posted on July 31, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1108 words  |  Aldo Pusey

 PARTY animals Lottie Moss and her Made In Chelsea pals Jamie Laing and Sophie Habboo just couldn’t wait for lockdown to be officially lifted before throwing a boozy bash.

So supermodel Kate’s little sister broke the Government’s rules anyway by attending a get-together thrown by the reality TV couple at their West London pad on Wednesday night.

It was clearly a rager as it continued until 5am on Thursday morning.

Former castmate Emily Blackwell and their influencer friend Bethany Moore joined the gang for drinks and nibbles in the garden for the drawn-out session, proudly pouting for their Instagram stories.

Neighbours in the posh area were not happy with the noise the group were making, and complained.

It comes after Lottie tired of isolating with her sister in the Cotswolds and craved a return to life in London. A source said:

“The group love to socialise and are used to being out and about most nights a week.

“They have really struggled being cooped up in lockdown.

“A low-key gathering at Jamie and Habs’ house got a bit out of hand with lots of booze and music.”

Former Strictly Come Dancing contestant Jamie had already turned his nose up at social distancing.

‘Little treats’

Last week he was snapped cycling with his reality TV mate Alex Mytton, who is Lottie’s ex. Lottie, who is currently single, previously dated former Made In Chelsea cast member Sam Prince and British actor Christian Cooke.

The model opened up about getting a boob job last year, but admitted she regretted getting lip injections after following the crowd.

Lottie said: “I did my boobs and I’m very happy with that. You’ve got to spend some money on your-self and you’ve got to treat yourself, and this is one of those little treats that women like to do.”

Speaking on social media last week, Lottie also claimed that she had been working on herself during the weeks of lockdown.

She added: “It’s been really nice, actually.

“I’ve spent time by myself and healing and working on myself, which has been amazing.

“I’ve been reading lots of self-help books and taking care of my skin a lot more. I’ve run every day.

“It’s really just a time to try and focus on yourself and better yourself, so that when you come out of this isolation you feel amazing, refreshed and rebooted.”

A little too refreshed, some might say.

Zara has room to spare

IT didn’t take long for Zara McDermott to take over boyfriend Sam Thompson’s spare room after moving in with him for lockdown.

The ex-Love Island star, currently fronting online retailer boohoo’s #boohoointhehouse social media campaign, needed somewhere to put all her clothes after nesting in the Made In Chelsea regular’s home.

It comes after she also sang on The X Factor: Celebrity and joined Sam for some tiffs with the toffs on E4’s MIC.

She said: “I convinced Sam to flatpack his bed and we’ve turned his second bedroom into a massive walk-in wardrobe. It’s great to be able to spread out and put a dressing table in there.

“I’ve also created a studio in his basement where I can record songs. He has been very accommodating.”

Let’s hope Sam sees it that way.

It's nifty Nigella

NIGELLA Lawson might be worth an estimated £16 million – but it doesn’t stop her being frugal.

The TV cook refuses to waste a morsel and has a wealth of thrifty kitchen hacks, from freezing bits of cheese to serving up spud peelings.

She said: “I freeze leftover milk, and cheese, including the Parmesan ends and rinds. I also freeze peelings of vegetables to make stock or soup. I make my potato-peeling crisps.”

On The Bunker podcast, she added: “I used gone-off milk to make bread the other day.” There’s a canny cookbook idea here.

Jon diva downer

Jonathan Ross says he is fed up with bending over backwards trying to please Hollywood actors.

The ITV chat show host, who loses out on many of the A-list stars to BBC rival Graham Norton, prefers welcoming down-to-earth British guests on to his sofa because they are less diva-ish.

He said: “I don’t like having movie stars on my show. The movie companies are a nightmare to deal with, especially American stars. You’ll speak to them beforehand and say, ‘We want to do this’ and then they’ll turn up on the day and say,

‘No you can’t talk about that any more’.

“On the day you can’t just change your mind.”

They probably don’t get his jokes either.
