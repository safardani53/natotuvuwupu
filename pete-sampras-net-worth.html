Pete Sampras: Career

Let us look at his tennis career in brief. Sampras participated in a professional match in 1988. Undoubtedly, his skills were exceptional; this is backed up by the fact that he rose from 893rd to 97th in the global rankings.

While playing in the Australian Open in 1990, he rose to world No. 5. In 1991, he won five career titles at the end of the Tennis Match Cup. After playing the Australian Open and the French Open and defeating Jim Courier, he became the world’s No.1.

Moreover, in 1995 Sampras retained his title by winning the U.S. Open. Again he won his previous title by winning 13 major titles in 2000. He did not win any titles in 2001, dropping to the world No.10, and in 2002, just before retirement, he won 14 titles.

Who once began as a young boy with an extreme passion for tennis became the world’s No.1 for almost five and half years. His journey is truly motivating.
