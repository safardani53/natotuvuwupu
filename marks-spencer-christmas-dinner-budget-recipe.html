You can make Christmas dinner for just 2 quid a person using M&amp;S buys - and it can all be prepped in

 Posted on July 24, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  468 words  |  Larita Shotwell

A SAVVY mum has shared how she's making Christmas dinner for six people at just £2 a head.

The money-saving woman took to social media to show off the dish she created using M&S groceries.

In the clip, she said: "Let me show you how to make an incredible Christmas dinner centrepiece for six people for less than £10.

"And it's using items that all come from Marks and Spencer.

"And what is even better is you can prep it in advance."

The dish also takes just one hour to cook in the oven - perfect for those wanting to enjoy Christmas day rather than being stuck in the kitchen all afternoon.

The first step was making stuffing balls using M&S stuffing mix and 400ml of hot water.

She let the mixture call before rolling into equal sized balls.

Next, she began the main course for the day using two packs of M&S chicken legs and removed the bones and skin.

She then laid them out on top of a slice of bacon and added some of the stuffing mixture to the centre of the chicken.

The mum then folded the chicken and bacon over the stuffing and tied it together using butcher string to create six individual parcels.

Keeping the bones to one side, she would cook them in a pan with vegetables and water to make a gravy to go with the dish.

The leftover chicken skin will also be used to cook with the roast potatoes for some extra flavour.

She continued: " I have also got leftovers of the stuffing, so I've made these into stuffing balls.

"And the sausages and bacon that were left over, I have turned into some extra pigs in blankets.

"You can now pop this into the fridge or the freezer, and it is ready for Christmas Day.

"But just look how incredible this is.

"Just £2 a head for Christmas dinner, all cooked in less than an hour."

she revealed the parcels can be cooked in 55 minutes at 190 in the oven - but always check the temperature before serving.

The clip posted to TikTok (@thismumcooks)has since gone viral with over 40k views and 1,000 likes.

People were seriously impressed with the dish and couldn't wait to give it a go.

One person wrote: "Not a great lover of turkey so this is a great alternative for me, thanks."

Another commented: "I'm always surprised at how much people say Christmas dinner costs, a roast never costs me that much. this looks great."

"This looks lovely," penned a third.
