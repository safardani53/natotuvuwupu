THIS snobby dog can sniff out an own-brand Wotsit from the real thing — even when his owner tries to trick him.

Footage shows "fussy little bugger" Patch cautiously smell each crisp when offered one by owner Dan Huby.

He carefully avoids the cheaper rival and only accepts the branded Walkers snacks.

Restaurant manager Dan, 31, even tried to trick his picky pet by swapping a Wotsit into a Cheese Puffs bag — but the snooty pooch was having none of it.

Although Wotsits are corn snacks and Cheese Puffs are made of maize, Dan admits he doesn’t think the rival brands taste much different.

Dan, from Ventnor on the Isle of Wight, said: “He loves Wotsits. I hardly get to eat a packet myself now. He’s always around when I’ve got a packet open.

“I’d just got back from work and I opened a packet of the Cheese Puffs while my missus had a bag of Wotsits."

He added: “Basically I threw him some of the Cheese Puffs and he wouldn’t go for them at all. He just started going for the Wotsits.

“I thought, OK, I’ll try this out a bit and see if I could trick him. It turns out he’s got a bit of a nose for the Wotsits.

“I tried to give him a cheese puff but he refused it and wanted the Wotsits. I just wondered, well what’s the difference?

“I’m not sure what he thinks is the difference between them. Me and my missus just started creasing."

The snacks may have just been a one-off treat but now Dan has found he has unexpected competition when eating a bag of the Wotsits himself.

Dan said: “I thought he was a fussy little bugger. He doesn’t get Wotsits a lot. They’re only a treat really.

“I prefer Wotsits as well though so we have to fight for them. He’s a good boy really though. He’s a bouncy ten-year-old with too much energy and he’s very gobby.

“He doesn’t know many other tricks but he’s just a funny little dog and likes to do barrel rolls and things like that.”
