Paul Duane Beauregard, known as DJ Paul, is an American DJ, record producer, rapper, musician and businessman from Memphis, Tennessee. He is a founding member of the hip hop group Three 6 Mafia and the brother of the late rapper Lord Infamous. DJ Paul started his career in the late 1980s as a DJ and has released several solo bands, as well as three albums of collaboration with Lord Infamous like The Serial Killaz. Juicy J was introduced and the three formed Three 6 Mafia, achieving nationwide success in the late 1990s and early 2000s. Their 2000 album, When the Smoke Clears: Sixty 6, Sixty 1, came out. debuted at number six on the Billboard 200 and was certified platinum. Then there were Da Unbreakables in 2003 and Most known Unknown in 2005, both commercially successful. In 2006, DJ Paul, Juicy J, Crunchy Black and Frayser Boy won the Oscar for Best Original Song for "It's Hard Out Here for a Pimp" from Hustle & Flow.
