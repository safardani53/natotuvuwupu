My 180sqft tiny house has 'everything that a regular house has' including a washer-dryer and it only

 Posted on July 15, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  561 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

A HOMEOWNER has shown that tiny homes can offer all of the same luxuries as a regular-sized home at a fraction of the cost.

Tiny homes have skyrocketed in popularity in recent years.

Contrary to what most people think, a tiny home can have everything that a regular home has, albeit on a smaller scale.

Jack (@jackleonardbirt) built his tiny home with all the necessary bells and whistles for just $22,800.

He has amassed over 755,800 followers on TikTok, where he shares his tips about "living large on a tiny budget."

In one of his most recent videos, he took his followers on a tour of his home and broke down the costs.

TINY HOME, TINY COSTS

Jack said that the home itself was $17,500.

It is 20 feet long, 9 feet wide, and has about 14-foot ceilings.

It is on wheels, but he has covered them to keep out any drafts.

He decided to take some inspiration from his home state of Colorado and decked out the outside with cedar wood and a metal part to mimic the design of a cabin.

On the inside the home was extremely spacious.

"It pretty much has everything that a regular house has," Jack said.

And he wasn't exaggerating. The home has a PC set up, an entire kitchen with a butcher block countertop, a microwave and microwave oven, and a fridge.

Jack custom-built all the shelving and storage for his kitchen with iron piping.

In the living room area, he made a couch that completely folds up into the wall so he can maximize the space when he needs it.

The bathroom might be the most impressive room in the home, with its large cedar wood shower that looks like it came straight out of a ski lodge.

Some people might not be a fan of his composting toilet, but he uses the compost as fertilizer on the land surrounding the home.

Jack has a two-in-one washer-dryer that saves some space.

The 14-foot ceilings allow enough space for Jack to build upwards and make a loft for him to sleep.

He made custom stairs to hide drawers in the steps where he keeps all his clothes.

In the bedroom area, he has a 100-inch screen projector for entertainment, a queen bed, and two air conditioning units.

He powers everything with solar units and has a rainwater collection system to get fresh drinking water.

DON'T DO IT YOURSELF

For anyone who might be inspired to build their own tiny home, there are a few important steps to take before breaking ground on the project.

First, it is essential to check local laws and regulations regarding what structures can be used as permanent homes in your area.

The rules vary based on county and city, but they usually require things like insulation, electricity, and plumbing.

There may also be size requirements for the home. For example, in Florida, there is a minimum square footage that all homes must meet.

It also pays to look into the different regulations regarding tiny homes on wheels, as they may be more lenient.
