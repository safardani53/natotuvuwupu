Who is GMA anchor Janai Norman?

Norman was born on January 5, 1990, making her 33 years old in 2023.

Janai Norman's love for journalism began from a young age, New York Amsterdam News reports.

She began taking classes in high school for journalism and also went on to become the editor-in-chief of her school newspaper in her junior and senior years, the outlet reports.

After high school, Norman attended the University of Missouri. While in college, she went to intern for NewsOne at ABC in New York City.

Eventually, Norman worked in both Oklahoma and Florida before landing back in New York at ABC.

Before her time at GMA, Norman worked as a multi-platform reporter for ABC. In 2019, she joined GMA for the second hour on Saturdays.

In May 2022, she appeared on the GMA and Hulu special Breaking the Mental Health Stigma for Black Women. Her work on GMA has largely focused on pop culture and celebrity news in recent years.

On her Instagram, where she has over 70K followers, Norman is often seen with some of the most famous faces in entertainment.

When did Janai Norman become a weekend anchor for GMA?

On July 5, 2022, it was revealed that Norman was announced as the new co-anchor for the weekend edition of Good Morning America.

She kicked off her new role on July 9, 2022.

In a staff memo obtained by The Hollywood Reporter ABC News president Kim Godwin revealed the news.

"Janai is a versatile and talented journalist, whether she is anchoring at the desk, conducting a one-on-one interview with a newsmaker of the day, or reporting in the field on breaking news," Godwin wrote.

"She puts her heart into her work and makes it a priority to cover the most important underreported issues of our time, including the maternal mortality crisis facing Black women and the stigma of mental health."
